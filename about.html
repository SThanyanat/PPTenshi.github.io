About

  • What is a VTuber?

    An online entertainer who uses a digital, computer-generated avatar instead of showing their real face, combining anime aesthetics with live streaming and video creation on platforms like YouTube, Twitch, and TikTok. They perform activities like gaming, singing, chatting, and storytelling, often maintaining distinct characters or backstories, with motion capture technology translating their real-time movements to the avatar. This trend, originating in Japan, allows creators to build a public persona while maintaining privacy, blending technology with Japanese pop culture.

  • What is Hololive?

    a VTuber management group that consists of the talent groups hololive and HOLOSTARS. hololive production has over 80 affiliated talents active in Japan, Indonesia, and English-speaking countries, and has over 80 million YouTuber subscribers all across the channels.
    With COVER Corporation’s VR/AR technology, hololive production talents provide various experiences ranging from game streams to offline concerts, and form a stable community that connects fans, creators, and talents all around the world via social networks.
